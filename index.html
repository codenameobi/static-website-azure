about us

Engr. George Chiedozie Oguejiofor (Ph.D) is a Teacher, a Trainer and a Development Chemical Engineer with Nnamdi Azikiwe University (NAU), Awka, Anambra State, where he teaches and develops prototypes engineering teaching and research equipment. Besides, Engr. Oguejiofor had taught in some secondary schools in Enugu State, and Institute of Management and Technology (IMT), Enugu. He was part of the authorship of the academic brief submitted to National Universities Commission (NUC) which drove the establishment of chemical engineering as an academic discipline in NAU, Awka. Born in Kaduna, Kaduna State, he earned his undergraduate and postgraduate degrees in accountancy, operations management and chemical engineering from University of Nigeria Enugu Campus (UNEC); Nnamdi Azikiwe University (NAU), Awka; and Enugu State University of Science and Technology(ESUT), Enugu.

Author's Message

services

Insufficient budgets and high foreign exchange rates were the major challenges confronting the importation of engineering teaching and research equipment. These gave rise to equipment shortages. To remedy the shortages and enhance the pedagogy of engineering students, over 10 homegrown prototypes engineering teaching and research equipment were developed in-house. The various prototypes' developments were carried out by a multi-disciplinary and gender-sensitive team of chemical, industrial and mechanical engineers led by me.
However ‘prototypes developments’ as the keyword aligned with: (a) process equipment development in chemical engineering discipline; and (b) product development in industrial engineering discipline. Talking the talk would not develop physical prototypes. So the talk was walked and the walk was walked and various prototypes were physically realised. The resulting prototypes were listed in the next section captioned, gallery of engineering teaching and research equipment.

So far the prototypes had accomplished the following.
(a) They had meaningfully enhanced chemical engineering pedagogy at NAU Awka, IMT Enugu and Caritas University Enugu.
(b) They had significantly improved the equipmentation of the laboratories in NAU's chemical engineering department, as well as chemical engineering laboratories at Caritas University Enugu and IMT Enugu.
(c) They were predominantly helpful in the successes of the various cycles of accreditation exercises for NAU's and Caritas University's chemical engineering discipline by:(i) National Universities Commission (NUC); and (ii) The Council for the Regulation of Engineering in Nigeria (COREN).
(d) Awka Branch of NSE conferred on me with merit award in recognition of my outstanding contribution to the development of the engineering education at NAU Awka because of the assorted prototypes development.
Welcome to the gallery of homegrown prototypes engineering teaching and research equipment.
